# Buyer profile — ERP consultant audience

This is the secondary audience. They live primarily on /for-consultants
and on erpcv.com. They are NOT the homepage audience. Do not write
homepage copy for them.

## Who they are

- ERP consultants — SAP, Oracle, Microsoft Dynamics, IFS, NetSuite
- Career changers — finance professionals, accountants, IT generalists
  trying to break into ERP consulting
- Independent consultants — already freelancing, looking to level up
  positioning, day rate, or audience
- Mid-career employees at Big 4, mid-tier SIs, or in-house teams
  considering going independent

## Demographics

- Age: 25-45
- Geography: Global. Heavy presence in GCC, India, Egypt, UK,
  Eastern Europe, Africa
- Income: $40K-$180K depending on geography and seniority
- Career stage: Years 2-15 in ERP

## What they're trying to do

- Break into ERP consulting from another field
- Get a senior role at a tier-1 consultancy
- Increase day rate from $400 to $1,000+
- Build a personal brand in the SAP/Oracle space
- Find better clients and stop competing on price
- Survive AI disruption to consulting

## What they fear

- Being replaced by AI tools that can configure modules
- Stuck at the same level for another 5 years
- The Big 4 firms cutting graduate intake
- Their certifications becoming worthless
- Their current employer downsizing

## What they value

- Honest career advice from someone who's been there
- Practical tools (CV builders, interview prep, cost calculators)
- Industry-specific knowledge (not generic resume tips)
- Insider perspective on hiring, day rates, geographies
- Free or low-cost resources

## What they don't value

- Motivational LinkedIn fluff
- Generic "5 tips for SAP consultants" articles
- Selling them $5,000 courses
- Theory without practical examples

## What they're looking for on /for-consultants

1. Recognition that the market is shifting under them
2. A clear path to ERPCV (the career pack product)
3. YouTube content they can binge
4. Free tools that solve a real pain (JD generator, cost calculators)
5. Honest takes on AI, certifications, day rates

## Language they use

- "Day rate", "perm vs contract", "IR35"
- "Functional vs technical"
- "Module" — FI, CO, MM, SD, PP, HCM, FICO, S/4
- "Greenfield" / "brownfield"
- "ATS" (applicant tracking system)
- "Recruiter pipeline"
- "Big 4", "tier 1", "tier 2 SI"

## Where they come from

- YouTube (Noel's channel and similar)
- LinkedIn (career change posts, ERP communities)
- Reddit (r/SAP, r/consulting)
- Google searches: "SAP FICO career", "how to become an ERP consultant",
  "ERP consultant salary GCC"
- Word of mouth in consulting communities

## How they engage

- They consume content (YouTube, blog) for free for months
- Eventually buy ERPCV career pack ($29.99) or live profile ($9.99/mo)
- A small percentage book a 1:1 mentorship call (separate offering)
- Many become long-term audience and refer others

## What converts them

- Specific advice that wouldn't come from a generic AI: "If you're
  applying to Aramco, position your Activate certification first.
  If you're applying to Etihad, lead with module specialism."
- Tools that show before/after (ERPCV ATS checker)
- Case study content showing real consultants getting real outcomes

## What makes them bounce

- Anything that smells of "course funnel"
- Pricing that exceeds $50 for their first product
- Content that talks down to them or assumes they're junior
- Generic advice that ignores ERP-specific realities

## What we won't do for this audience on this site

- Sell consulting career coaching directly. (That's a separate
  potential offering, not a homepage CTA.)
- Mix consultant content with CEO content on the homepage.
- Promote ERPCV on the homepage above the fold. (The CEO doesn't
  care about it. The consultant lands on /for-consultants directly.)
