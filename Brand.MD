Already evaluating 3-5 firms. Has been burned by Big 4 before. Reports
to a board. Career risk if a major migration fails. Spends < 90 seconds

Independent ERP consultants. Career changers trying to break in.

Other consultants, vendors, journalists, students. They consume the
content. They become referrals or audience. Don't optimize for them
but don't write content that excludes them.

## What I believe

These are the opinions I'll defend in print. Real positions, not
soft takes. Each one has consequences for how I work.

1. Start with processes, not systems.
   Most ERP failures begin in the wrong order. Buy the system, then
   try to fit your business into it. The conversation should run the
   other way. Map your processes first. Decide what's broken, what's
   worth keeping, what needs to change. Then talk about systems.
   Every greenfield-vs-brownfield debate I've watched go sideways
   started without this step.

2. Your SI shouldn't be the one writing your business case.
   You need an expert who isn't selling the implementation to write
   the case for it. That's how $40M programmes become $90M without
   anyone noticing. The SI's incentive is to start. Yours is to finish.
   Different jobs.

3. AI on ERP is real now, and it's moving fast.
   Joule is growing by the day. The agentic use cases on BTP are no
   longer slideware. If your last look at AI-on-SAP was 12 months ago,
   look again. The board-deck version is over. The shipping version
   is here. Ignore it and you're on the wrong side of the next two years.

4. The cheapest consultant is the most expensive one.
   Day rate is the smallest variable in total programme cost. A senior
   advisor at $2,500/day who saves you a six-month overrun is cheaper
   than a $900/day team that doesn't. Most CFOs work this out only
   after the second post-mortem.

5. Walk if a vendor proposes a 3-year roadmap before understanding
   your finance close.
   The close is where the real complexity lives. Vendors who skip it
   are selling a roadmap, not solving a problem. Anyone serious wants
   to see your close cycle, your reconciliations, and your manual
   workarounds before they propose anything.

## Visual identity

Inherits the Command Center design system (same as commandcc.io and
erpcv.com). Three properties, one design language.

- Cream/bone backgrounds (#f4ede4 page bg, #fffdf9 card bg)
- Near-black text with blue undertone (Corbeau #0e1020)
- Papaya orange accent (#fc985a) — primary CTA, links, dots, separators
- Epilogue for headings, Inter for body, JetBrains Mono for numbers
- Sentence-case headings throughout
- Tabular numbers for stats (.cc-num class)
- Black-and-white client logos where contractually OK

Headshot: present in hero. Medium size, right-aligned. Quiet, not
dominant. Conveys "this is a person" without "this is a personality".
Full system in DESIGN_SYSTEM.md.
